# Trademark & naming policy

The **code and profiles are open** (Apache-2.0 / CC-BY-4.0) — you may fork, use,
modify and redistribute them freely. The **name "Credential Commons" and the
project logo are not** covered by those licences; they identify this project and
its official outputs.

This is the normal open-source split (cf. Linux, Python, etc.): open substance,
protected identity. It protects adopters as much as us — a "Credential Commons
conformant" claim should mean something.

**You may**, without asking:
- say your data/tool "works with", "is based on", or "is conformant to"
  Credential Commons profiles;
- use the name factually in documentation, talks and articles.

**Please ask first** (open an issue) before:
- naming a fork, product or service "Credential Commons" or a confusingly
  similar name;
- using the logo as your own product's mark;
- implying official endorsement or certification.

Conformance is defined only by the published profiles in this repository: if
`cc validate` passes for a given profile version, the data is conformant to that
version. There is no separate paid gate to *being conformant*; hosted validation
and support at credentialstudy.com are a convenience, not a requirement.
